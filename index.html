Welcome to Bucharest

Guide for cheap and awesome city

Bucharest is the hidden gem of Eastern Europe. Warm and soft climate, awesome food, very chilled atmosphere, mountains and the sea only in a bit more than 2 hours ride make this city a perfect travel destination. Check how cheap it is!

Palace of Parliament

or the People’s House

The biggest building in Europe and the symbol of the Communist era is now available for walking tours. The Inside of the building is even more impressive — everything is made of Romanian materials and the longest tour will take you from the air terrace to the basement, where Ceausescu and Jeremy Clarkson rode their cars.

Longest tour:

55 lei

Taxi

Uber or Yellow Cab

Taxis are usually cheap. Just look at their side doors for the price. Sometimes drivers won’t turn on their devices, directly trying to negotiate a total price with you. For not to get into troubles, it is recommended to call for a taxi or to order one by mobile app.

Average ride:

10 lei

Covrigul

Romanian pretzel

At almost every corner of Bucharest you can buy cheap and tasty covrigul — The Romanian kind of pretzel or Simit. It is the most popular street food here and in common versions it comes with sesame, poppy, sunflower seeds and raisins.

1 covrigul:

1 lei

Metro

One ticket valid for two trips

The metro was built during Communist era to take people from the suburbs to their working places. Now you can enjoy a ride with a book to one of the shopping malls or go to the station “Politehnica” and check the Jurrassic Period molluscs inside the marble.

1 ticket for 2 trips:

5 lei

Palatul Primaverii

Former house of Ceausescu

If you want to see how the leader lived, while ruling Romania over 24 years — go and visit his former residence. You are going to see his flat, his swimming pool, an indoor and outdoor garden, a private cinema and the “golden bathroom”, which is not really golden. The museum opened only in 2016, so be one of the first tourist to come here.

English speaking tour:

45 lei

Shaorma

They add fries into the kebab!

The common name for kebab in Bucharest is Shaorma. There are fancy ones, expensive ones, cheap ones and the ones where you are getting your meat inside a ciabatta. Pay attention that they are always adding fries inside and definitely try the "piquant"(spicy) sauce.

Average small shaorma:

11 lei

Filarmonica George Enescu

Music in the most beautiful building of the town

The Philharmonic Orchestra was named after the great Romanian composer George Enescu, who played his shows inside the Romanian Athenaeum. The Inside of the building is even more beautiful, so it is worth to check out, even if you are not a fan of classical music. Sparkling wine inside is also not very expensive.

Average ticket:

30 lei

Espresso

Drink it in the morning

Romanian people love coffee and drink tea only in winter. There are: hipster coffee places, fancy coffee places, ugly coffee places and shops with Tchibo machines with the cheapest coffee of the town.

Average price:

6 lei

Constanta

If you need water - it’s here

The only one thing that is missing in Bucharest — is the water. And when there is hot summer and you want to refresh yourself lying by the sea with extremely cheap Aperol Spritz — you may come to Constanta. When you get tired of swimming and sunbathing, check out the beautiful Art Nouveau Casino.

Average return ride:

100 lei
